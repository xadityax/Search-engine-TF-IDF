                                Prof. Aranya Bhuti Bhattacharjee


Current Position: Professor of Physics, BITS Pilani, Hyderabad Campus  since July 2018. Contact Details: Department of Physics, Birla Institute of Technology & Science, PilaniHyderabad Campus,Jawahar Nagar, Kapra Mandal, Medchal District,Hyderabad 500 078, Telangana, IndiaEmail: aranyabhuti@hyderabad.bits-pilani.ac.inPhone:  +91 - 4066303587  Previous positions: UGC-Associate Professor, School of Physical Sciences, Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi, (2013-2018). Associate Professor, Department of Physics, ARSD College, University of Delhi, (1993-2013). Education:  ●         BSc (Honours) in Physics: 1985-1988, Ramjas College,  University of Delhi, India  ●         MSc Physics: 1988-1990, University of Delhi, India  ●  PhD Physics: 1990-1995: University of Delhi, India, Thesis Title: Physical Studies on the Molecular Organization and Dynamics of some Bio-Molecules in some Body Disorders. Awards and Research accomplishments:  ØJunior research fellowship of the University Grants Commission, India. ØFormer Associate of the following research institutes: 1. The Abdus Salam International Center for Theoretical Physics, Trieste, Italy. 2. Jawaharlal Nehru center for advanced scientific research, Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, India. 3. Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai, India. ØAwarded the ICTP-TRIL fellowship for one year post-doc with Prof. E. Arimondo, University of Pisa, Italy (February 2003-2004). ØAwarded the special research grant of the “German Science Foundation”(2005) to visit the research group of Prof. Robert Graham, University of Essen, Germany. ØAwarded the short term fellowship (2006) of DAAD, Germany to visit the research group of Prof. Robert Graham, University of Essen, Germany. ØAwarded the Endeavour Research Fellowship of the Australian Government for a 6 month research position at the Nonlinear Physics Center, Australian National University (2007), Group of Professor Yuri Kivshar.. ØAwarded 15 months Guest Scientist position at the Max Planck Institute for Physics of Complex Systems, Dresden, Germany (2007-2008). ØAwarded a grant from Weierstras-Institute for Angewandte Analysis and Stochastik, Berlin, in 2008 to work as Guest Scientist with Dr. Monika Pietrzyk. ØVisited Max Planck Institute for Physics of complex systems, Dresden, Germany 2009 as guest scientist. ØAwarded a grant from Weierstras-Institute for Angewandte Analysis and Stochastik, Berlin, in 2009 to work as Guest Scientist with Dr. Monika Pietrzyk. ØAwarded the short term fellowship (2011) of DAAD, Germany to visit the research group of Dr. Axel Pelster, Freie University, Berlin, Germany. ØVisited the research group of Prof. Tobias Brandes, Technical University of Berlin, Germany under DFG grant, June 2012. ØVisited the research group of Prof. Axel Pelster, Technical University of Kaiserslautern, Germany under DAAD grant, June 2015. ØSelected as University Grants Commission-Associate Professor under the UGC-Faculty Recharge Programme. ØAppointed as Research Ambassador for German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) 2015. ØGiven the honor of “Speaker of Eminence” at Indian Institute of Technology-BHU 2015. ØRecipient of the “Bharat Vikas Award”, 2016.  ØSuccessfully completed epg-pathshala online course of MHRD on Solid State Physics. The course is now available on the following website: https://epgp.inflibnet.ac.in/ahl.php?csrno=28  Research Supervision: PhD Fellows: 1.                  Tarun Kumar (PhD awarded). 2.                  Sonam Mahajan (PhD awarded). 3.                  Neha Aggarwal (PhD awarded). 4.                  Deepti Sharma (Currently pursuing). 5.                  Madhav Singh (Currently pursuing). 6.                  ShahNoor Ali (Currently pursuing). 7.                  Vaibhav Prakash (Currently pursuing)

Research Interest










                                Research Interest


Current research interest:
1.   Physics and applications of light-matter (ultra-cold atoms, ions, quantum dots in semi-conductor micro-cavities, thermal atoms) interaction
2.    Equilibrium and Non-equilibrium properties of Bose-Einstein condensates
3.      Dynamics of low dimensional systems such as quantum dots
4.  Quantum Optics and Quantum Opto-Mechanics: Quantum entanglement as application in quantum communication plateform
 
      5.    Nonlinear Optics: Application to Ring Laser Gyroscopes (Inertial navigation System) 
6.       Mathematical modeling of Bio-Films. 
 Other research interest: Astrobiology: The origin, evolution of life in the Universe.
