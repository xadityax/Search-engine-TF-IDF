                                Dr. Vivek Sharma




Assistant Professor
Department of Biological Sciences
B-209, BITS Pilani-Hyderabad Campus
Jawahar Nagar, Kapra Mandal, Medchal district
Hyderabad- 500 078, Telangana. 
Email: viveksharma@hyderabad.bits-pilani.ac.in, viveksharmabt@gmail.com
Phone: +91-40-66303627
 

About the Faculty
 
Work  Experience
Assistant Professor– BITS-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, India, May 2017.
DBT-Ramalingaswamy Fellow – IMS, BHU, Varanasi, India, Dec 2016 – Apr 2017.
Research Scientist – Premas Biotech Pvt. Ltd., Gurugram, India, Sep 2016 – Nov 2016.
Khorana Nirenberg Fellow – National Cancer Institute, NIH, USA, Aug 2011 – Aug 2016. Post-Doctoral Fellow – Ontario Institute of Cancer Research, Canada, Mar 2010 – Jun 2011. 
 
Education           
      Ph.D. : National Brain Research Centre, Manesar, India, 2010
       B. Tech-M. Tech (Int.): Bio-Technology, Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University, Delhi, 2005.
 
Fellowships 
      Ramalingaswamy Re-Entry Fellowship, DBT India.
      Khorana-Nirenberg-Indo-US Research Career Transition Award, NIH-DBT.
      Senior Research Fellowship, CSIR, India.
      Junior Research Fellowship, NBRC, India.
 
Honors 
      NCI Director's Innovation
Program - Career Development Award 2014.
      Silver medal for securing second position in
M.Tech for the year 2004-2005.
      Certificate of merit
by GGSIPU, Delhi for coming third in All India Combined Entrance Test 2000.

Research Interest










                                Research Interest


One of the biggest realization in the field of genome biology during the last decade has been that the human genome is pervasively transcribed and codes for a large number of non protein coding but functional transcripts called long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs). They can act as both cis- and trans-regulators of gene activity and play important functional roles in almost all cellular processes by interacting with RNA binding proteins(RBPs).  My lab is interested in understanding the role of lncRNAs and their associated RBPs in maintenance of genome stability, regulation of gene expression in cancer and modulation of  host responses upon infection with Japanese Encephalitis Virus (JEV). These studies will provide insights into basic biological mechanisms and lay the foundation for development  of  novel diagnostic and treatment approaches for human diseases.
