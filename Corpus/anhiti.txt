                                Dr. Anhiti Patnaik


Ph.D. Cultural Studies (Trent University), M.Phil. Literature (Delhi University), M.A. English (St. Stephen's College)Research Interests: Victorian Literature, Crime Fiction and Criminology, Queer Theory, Trauma Studies, Postmodernism, Intersections between Law and Literature.   Anhiti Patnaik is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences. She defended her doctoral dissertation “Nineteenth-Century Aesthetics of Murder: Jack the Ripper to Dorian Gray” in May 2018 at the Department of Cultural Studies, Trent University in Canada. Her research examines how violence was aesthetically represented in the Victorian Age, with a focus on Charles Baudelaire, Thomas De Quincey, and Oscar Wilde. It also investigates the ethical position of the reader and the traumatic nature of our attraction to graphic representations of misogyny and violence.  Anhiti is an Ontario Trillium Scholar and Fellow of The Institute of World Literature, Harvard University and The School of Criticism and Theory, Cornell University. She taught English at Lady Shri Ram College for Women and Khalsa College at Delhi University before pursuing higher studies abroad. She is a prolific Wilde scholar and her essays and reviews have been published in international journals like Neo-Victorian Studies, Victorian Network, Bronte Studies and Bloomsbury's upcoming anthology Horror in the Global South.She may be contacted at:anhitipatnaik@hyderabad.bits-pilani.ac.in Consultation chamber. K 109Telephone ext. 901   Curriculum Vitae Institutional Duties: Library Committee Member, 2020-presentCurator, Reference Library and Reading Room, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences Cultural Studies Course Handout, Work Integrated Learning Programme (WILP) - designed in collaboration with Dr. Muhammed P. Afzal (Pilani Campus)Faculty Advisor, ELAS Virtual Magazine

                                Workshops

                                Guest Lectures 


November 22, 2019- GS F242 Cultural Studies- Invited Sugandha Sehgal, Assistant Professor, University of Delhi to lecture on "Social Media Feminism in Contemporary Indian Art" which examined the female body as a site of corporeal and cultural protest on Instagram. May 8, 2020 - GSF242 - Invited Prof. Michael Puett, Walter C. Klein Professor of Chinese History and Anthropology, Harvard University to lecture on "Ritual, Sincerity, and Human Flourishing: Theories from Classical China" which drew from the philosophical thought of Zhuangzi and Xunzi and applied to it ethical behaviour and ritual pratice in the contemporary world.
