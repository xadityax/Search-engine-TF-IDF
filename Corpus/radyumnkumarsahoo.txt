About the Faculty
P. K. Sahoo earned his Ph.D. degree in Mathematics from Sambalpur University, Sambalpur, India in Jan. 2004.

Award/Fellowship Received: 

1. Fellow of Institute of Mathematics & its Applications (FIMA), London

2. Fellow of Royal Astronomical Society (FARS), London
3. Elected as a foreign
member of the Russian Gravitational Society

4. Reviewer excellence award 2019 by Indian Academy of Sciences, India  
 
5. Science Academies Summer Research Fellowship-2014.
6. UGC Visiting Fellow, Sardar Patel University during the academic year 2015-2016. 
Professional Background
 
Associate Professor, Birla Institute of Technology & Science-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, since 01.02.2015
Assistant Professor, Birla Institute of Technology & Science-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, 02.07.2009-31.01.2015
Lecturer, Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, Ranchi, 04.04.2007-30.06.2009
Lecturer, The Techno School, Bhubaneswar, Orissa, 23.08.2006-31.03.2007 
Lecturer, Padmashree Krutartha Acharya College of Engineering, Bargarh, Orissa, 22.09.2002-18.08.2006

                                Research Interest



Research interests
include Relativity, Cosmology, Dark Energy, Dark Matter, Wormhole Geometry and
Modified Theories of Gravity. He has published more than 100 papers in
reputed journals like Physical Review D, Physics of the Dark Universe, European Physical Journal C,
International Journal of Modern Physics D, International Journal of Modern
Physics A, Fortschritte der Physik, Modern Physics Letters
A, General Relativity & Gravitation, Gravitation & Cosmology, Results in Physics, European Physical
Journal Plus, Astrophysics & Space Science, Indian Journal of Physics etc.

                                Seminars





Co-convenor of the workshop Structure formation in Standard cosmology orgnised by Department of Mathematics, BITS-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus in association with IUCAA, Pune during 19-23 Dec. 2016. 
 

Local organizing committee member of 30th meeting of the Indiana Association for General Relativity and Gravitation (IAGRG) (Jan 3 -5, 2019)
 

Invited
Talks
 

15. IWARA 2020 Video Conference- 9th International workshop on Astronomy and Relativistic Astrophysics at Mexico from 6th – 12th Sept. 2020 and gave a talk on “Energy Conditions in Non-minimally Coupled f(R,T) Gravity”.
 

14. The 14th International Conference on Gravitation, Astrophysics and Cosmology (ICGAC14) at National Central University, Jhongli, Taiwan from 17th -21st  Aug. 2020 and gave an invited talk through webinar on “A non-singular bounce in f(R,T) gravitation”.

 
13. National Conference on emerging trends in advanced mathematical sciences & its interdisciplinary areas (NCETAMSII2020) at Department of Mathematical Sciences, Bodoland University, India from 21st -22nd Feb. 2020 and gave an invited talk on “Modified gravity wormholes without exotic matter”. 
 
12. International Conference on Advances in Physical, Chemical & Mathematical Sciences (ICAPCM-2020) at Department of Mathematics, RTM  Nagpur University, India from 13th -16th Feb. 2020 and gave an invited talk on “Bouncing cosmology in modified gravity”. 
 
11. International Conference on Differential Geometry and Relativity-2019 (ICDGR-2019) at Department of Mathematics and Statistics, Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Gorakhpur University, Gorakhpur, India from 8th -10th Nov. 2019 and gave an invited talk on “Wormholes in Modified Gravity and General Relativity: A Comparative Study”.
 
10. International Conference on Gravitation, Cosmology and Mathematical Physics-2019 (ICGCAMP-2019) at Department of Mathematics and Physics, GLA University, Mathura, India from 4th -7th April 2019 and gave an invited talk on “Finite time future singularities in modified f(R) gravity”. 
 
9. National Conference on Cryptography, Thermoelasticity and General Relativity (CTGR-2019) at Department of Mathematics, Nagpur University, Nagpur, India on 27th March 2019 and gave an invited talk on “Static spherically symmetric wormholes in f(R,T) extended theory of gravity”. 
 
8. International Conference on Recent Inventions and Innovations in Mathematical Sciences-2019 (ICRIIMS-2019) at Department of Applied Mathematics, Andhra University, Visakhapatnam, India from 28th Feb.-1st March 2019 and gave an invited talk on “Wormholes with different redshift functions in f(R,T) gravity”. 
 


7. Frontiers in Particle Physics and Cosmology – 2019 (FPPC2019) at School of Physics, University of Hyderabad, Hyderabad, India from 23-25 Jan 2019 and gave an invited talk on "Traversable wormholes and energy conditions in f(R,T) gravity".

 
6. International Conference on recent advances in pure and applied mathematics (ICRAPAM2018) at Department of Applied Mathematics , Delhi Technological University, Delhi, India from 23-25 October 2018 and gave an invited talk on “Traversable wormholes in modified gravity”.  
 
5. National Conference on Differential Geometry and General Relativity at Department of Mathematics, Sardar Patel University, Vallabh Vidyanagar, Gujarat, India from 28-29 November 2017 and gave an invited talk on “Wormhole Geometry in Modified Gravity”. 
 
4. International Conference on Mathematical Analysis and its Applications (ICMAA-2017) from 5-9 March, 2017 at Dayanand Science College, Latur-413512, Maharashtra, India gave an invited talk on "Big Rip singularity in f(R,T) gravity".
 

3. 3rd Conference of the Polish Society on Relativity  from 25th Sept. to 29th Sept. 2016 at Faculty of Physics, Astronomy and Applied Computer Science, Jagiellonian University, Krakow, Poland gave an invited talk on "Kaluza-Klein
dark energy model in the form of wet dark fluid in f(R,T) gravity". 

 
2. Frontiers in High Energy Physics from 22-25 March 2016 at Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai and gave an invited talk on "Big Rip singularity of LRS Bianchi type-I universe in f(R,T) gravity".


1. 20th European Workshop on “The String Theory Universe”, from Sept 22-26, 2014 at Mainz Institute for Theoretical Physics, Johannes Gutenberg University, Mainz, Germany and gave an invited talk on "Higher dimensional Bianchi type-III universe with strange quark matter attached to string cloud in general relativity".
