Profile










                                Dr Karthik Chethan V



Education: Ph.D., Materials Science and Engineering, McMaster University, Canada (2006). 
                   M. S., Materials Science and Engineering, University of Utah, USA (1998).
                   B. E., Polymer Science and Technology, University of Mysore, India (1994). 
 
Experience: Assistant Professor, BITS-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus (2012-present)
                     Research Scientist, Pittsburg State University, KS, USA (2010-2011). 
                     Research Scientist, Kansas State University, KS, USA (2008-2010).
                     Postdoctoral Research Associate, Kansas State University, KS, USA (2007-2008).
                     Research Engineer, Bodycote, Inc., IL, USA (1999-2001).

 Contact Details:  Assistant Professor 
                             Room No: D220, Department of Chemical Engineering
                             BITS-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, India. 
Email:  kvenk@hyderabad.bits-pilani.ac.in, kvenk@yahoo.com linkedin url: www.linkedin.com/pub/karthik-venkateshan/24/8b1/8a4

                                Sponsored Projects


SPONSORED PROJECTS: 1. Development of 3-D Printer Filaments that are Bio-material based and Eco-friendly. The project is sponsored by Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC), a Government of India Enterprise under the Biotechnology Ignition Grant (BIG) scheme. The approved grant amount is INR Rs. 34 Lakhs for a duration of eighteen months. THESIS PROJECTS:  1. Development of Bio-based formulations for electrospinning in obtaining nanofibers for encapsulation applications. The project will be conducted in collaboration with Dr. Chandra Shekhar Sharma, Dept. of Chemical Engineering, IIT-Hyderabad.

Engineering Chemistry
 Materials Science and Engineering
 WILP Course-Engineering Materials Chemical Engineering Laboratory - I Chemical Process Technology  Thermodynamics Laboratory Oriented Project Course (topics include Polymerization kinetics, Investigation of polymerization induced phase separation, Development of value-added products from biomaterials and Development of biomaterial formulations for microencapsulation applications) Study Oriented Project Course (topics include Development and characterization of biocomposites, Thermodynamic studies of protein and pharmaceutical interactions, Carbon nanotubes for flow sensor applications, Relaxational behavior of complex systems, Amorphization of pharmaceuticals and Development and interfacial characterization of carbon fiber based composites)  Guest lectures provided for course titled Bio-conversion Technology and offered by Dept. of Biological Sciences

Development of Biomaterial based formulations for 3-D printing and electrospinning techniques. The work involves product development and process optimization for adhesive/binder, food/gels, 3-D printing, microencapsulation and tissue engineering applications Real-time investigation of processes such as, polymerization, protein denaturation, liquid-liquid phase separation, glass formation, annealing, physical aging and corresponding secondary relaxation processes by using dielectrics, calorimetry, ultrasonics and thermal conductivity techniques. 
Materials characterization by using dielectrics, specific heat spectroscopy, dynamic mechanical spectroscopy, rheological spectroscopy, ultrasonics, thermal conductivity and electron microscopy techniques in characterizing a range of materials or products such as, polymers, biopolymers, gels, emulsions and colloids, coatings and adhesives, advanced composites etc..
