Profile










                                Dr. Biswanath Dash


 I did my M. Phil (2000-2002) and PhD (2002-2012) from Centre for Studies in Science Policy (CSSP), School of Social Science, JNU, New Delhi. My PhD work focused on 'Meteorology' as a 'Complex Science' and explored its uptake by the general Public during tropical cyclones. It situates 'evacuation' within 'science-society' engagement, highlighting inherent limitation of the underlying science' to explain  public action. My overall research seeks to bring together theoretical frameworks from ‘Science and Technology Studies’ with that of ‘Disaster Studies’.   I worked as a Research Officer at LBSNAA, Mussoorie (2005-2006), as an Assistant Professor at National Institute of Disaster Management, Government of India (2006-12) and in the same position  at Tata Institute of Social Science, Mumbai (2012-15). I carry teaching experience of over seven years and in addition have trained senior and middle level government officers for  over seven years. I have contributed to various policy papers, formulation of national disaster management guidelines and have taken up research and consultancy projects with organizations such as UNDP, International Federation of Red Cross, World Bank etc. Contact Details:biswanath@hyderabad.bits-pilani.ac.in 040-66303695 91-7036026393
