Research Interest










                                Research Interest


I am particularly enthusiastic about design and modelling of emerging post-CMOS devices like Tunnel FETs for label free gas-sensing and bio-sensing applications using numerical device simulation. My research emphasizes on the analysis and modelling of the underlying physics of transduction mechanism as well as the device electrostatics and electronic carrier transport of the sensors devices.  At the same time, I am interested in exploring and engineering the electronic and optical properties of two dimensional crystalline semiconductors for electronic, optoelectronic and sensing applications using first principle calculations. In the course of this research, I also collaborate with experimental groups and complement their findings with theoretical analysis based on density functional theory and quantum transport simulations.   Numerical Simulation packages used by our research group-  Theoretical Modelling: MATLABClassical/Semi-Classical Device Simulation: Sentaurus TCAD First Principle (Density Functional Theory) Calculation: ATK QuantumWise and Quantum Espresso with BuraiNano-scale Device (Quantum Transport) Simulation: Nano TCAD Vides  I am looking for full time self-sponsored (UGC/CSIR/DST) PhD candidates who are interested to work in any of the aforementioned areas.
